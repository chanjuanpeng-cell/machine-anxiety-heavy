Machine Anxiety

2026 — Single-channel video + real-time WebGL system — 54 seconds

A data-driven moving image in which a private interior is destabilised by historical GBP/CNY exchange-rate fluctuations.

Machine Anxiety translates macroeconomic volatility into spatial instability. Using exchange-rate data recorded between September 2024 and April 2026, the work drives a LiDAR-scanned model of the artist’s student accommodation.

As the data shifts, the space trembles, rotates, and gradually loses coherence, revealing how distant financial systems exert pressure on intimate environments.

System Logic

The work operates as a closed computational system where financial data acts as a continuous force rather than visual information.

Exchange-rate values are mapped to spatial deformation, rotation speed, camera movement, and particle density, producing a behavioural transformation of space rather than a direct visualisation of data.

Technical

  • Custom Three.js / WebGL system
  • Point-cloud environment reconstructed from LiDAR scan (~300k vertices)
  • Historical GBP/CNY exchange-rate dataset structured as JSON
  • Offline frame-by-frame rendering via CCapture.js
  • Final composition and sound editing in Adobe Premiere Pro
