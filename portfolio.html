Machine Anxiety

2026 — Single-channel video + real-time WebGL system

A data-driven moving image in which a private interior is destabilised by historical GBP/CNY exchange-rate fluctuations.

Machine Anxiety translates macroeconomic volatility into spatial instability. Using exchange-rate data recorded between September 2024 and April 2026, the work drives a LiDAR-scanned point-cloud model of the artist’s student accommodation.

As the data shifts, the space trembles, rotates, sounds, and gradually loses coherence, revealing how distant financial systems exert pressure on intimate environments.

The current preview system maps exchange-rate deviation, velocity, and shock into spatial deformation, camera pressure, point-cloud instability, and layered Web Audio. Anxiety is treated not as an internal emotion, but as a computational pressure acting on the room.

System Logic

The work operates as a closed computational system where financial data acts as a continuous force rather than visual information.

The updated real-time system calculates anxiety through three related signals: deviation from a baseline, velocity between recorded exchange-rate points, and shock caused by abrupt change. These signals drive spatial deformation, rotation speed, camera movement, point-cloud density, and a layered audio system.

The result is a behavioural transformation of space rather than a direct visualisation of data. The room does not display the exchange rate; it absorbs it.

Technical

  • Custom Three.js / WebGL system
  • Point-cloud environment reconstructed from LiDAR scan (~300k vertices)
  • Historical GBP/CNY exchange-rate dataset structured as JSON
  • Data mapping based on deviation, velocity, and shock
  • Layered Web Audio system driven by the same data signals
  • Branch-based versioning with Vercel preview deployments
  • Offline frame-by-frame rendering via CCapture.js
  • Final composition and sound editing in Adobe Premiere Pro
